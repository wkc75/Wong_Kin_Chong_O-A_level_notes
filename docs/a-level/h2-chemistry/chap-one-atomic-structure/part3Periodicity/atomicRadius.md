# **Periodic Trend in Atomic Radii**

## **Down the Group**

<div id="group-one-atomic-radius"></div>

Down the group

- The **nuclear charge increases** as the **proton number increases**.
- The **shielding effect increases** as the **number of inner electrons increases**.
- Therefore the **effective nuclear charge remains relatively constant**.
- As the **number of principal quantum shells increases**, the outermost electrons are **further away** from the nucleus.
- Hence the atomic radius increases.

## **Across Period 2 and 3**

<div id="period-two-atomic-radius"></div>

Across Period 2

- The **number of quantum shells** remain constant across the period.
- **Nuclear charge increases** as the **proton number increases**.
- **Shielding effect remain relatively constant** as the electrons are added to the same outermost shell.
- **Effective nuclear charge increases**, resulting in stronger electrostatic forces of attraction between the nucleus and the outermost electron.
- **Outermost electrons are pulled closer to the nucleus** and hence the atomic radius decreases.

## **Across the first row of Transition Elements**

Across the first row of transition elements

- **Number of quantum shells remains constant**
- **Nuclear charge increases** due to increasing number of electrons.
- **Electrons are added** to the **inner 3d subshell**, which **contributes** to the **shielding effect**.
- Thus shield effect increases, **nullifying the increase in nuclear charge due to every additional proton**.
- The effective nuclear charge ramins almost **constant**.
