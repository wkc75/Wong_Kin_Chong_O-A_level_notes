# **Ionic Radius**

## **Down the Group**

<div id="group-one-ionic-radius"></div>

Down the group

- The **nuclear charge increases** as **proton number increases**
- The **shielding effective increases** as **number of inner electrons increases**
- Thus, effective nuclear charge **remains relatively constant**.
- As the **number of quantum shell increases**, ionic radius increases.

## **Across Period 2 and 3**

<div id="period-three-ionic-radius"></div>

- The ions, $Na^-$, $Mg^{2+}$, $Al^{3+}$ and $Si^{4+}$ are said to be **isoelectronic** as they have the **same number of electrons**.
- Similarly, $P^{3-}$, $S^{2-}$ and $Cl^-$ are also **isoelectronic**.
- Across both isoelectronic series, the **number of quantum shells reamins constant**.
- The **nuclear charge increases** as the **number of protons increases**.
- The **shielding effect remains constant** as the **number of electrons remains constant**.
- Therefore, the **effective nuclear charge increases**, resulting in a **stronger attraction between the outermost electrons and the nucleus**.
- Thus, the **ionic radius decreases across both isoelectronic series**

There is a **sharp increase in ionic radius** from the cation series to the anion series. This is because the anions have one more quantum shell than the cations.
