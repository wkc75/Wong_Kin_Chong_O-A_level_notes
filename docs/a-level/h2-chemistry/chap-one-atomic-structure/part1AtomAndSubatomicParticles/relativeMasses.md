# **Relative Masses**

|      | Learning Outcomes                                                                              |
| ---- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 6(a) | define the terms relative atomic, isotopic, molecular and formula mass                         |
| 6(c) | calculate the relative atomic mass of an element given the relative abundances of its isotopes |

## **Relative Atomic Mass**

$$
Relative \; atomic \; mass, A_{r} = \; \frac{average \; mass \; of \; one \; atom \; of \; an \; element}{\frac{1}{12} \; \times \; the \; mass \; of \; one \; atom \; of \; ^{12}C}
$$

The **relative mass of atom of an element** is the ratio of the average mass of one atom of the element to $\frac{1}{12}$ times the mass of one atom of $^{12}C$

## **Relative Isotopic Mass**

$$
Relative \; isotopic \; mass, \; A_{r} \; = \; \frac{average \; mass \; of \; an \; isotope \; of \; an \; element}{\frac{1}{12} \; \times \; the \; mass \; of \; one \; atom \; of \; ^{12}C}
$$

The **relative mass of an isotope of an element** is the ratio of the average mass of an isotope of the element to $\frac{1}{12}$ times the mass of one atom of $^{12}C$

## **Relative Abundance of Isotopes**

The percentage of a particular isotope compared to the total number of atoms of that element in a mixture of isotopes is called the **isotopic abundance** or the **relative abundance**.

$$
Relative \, Atomic \, Mass, \, A_{r} \; = \; \sum (relative \; isotopic \; mass \; \times \; isotopic \; abundance)
$$
