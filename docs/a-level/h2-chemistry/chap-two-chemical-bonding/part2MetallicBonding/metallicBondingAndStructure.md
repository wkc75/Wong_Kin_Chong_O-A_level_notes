# **Metallic Bonding and Structure**

## **Giant Metallic Lattice Structure and Metallic Bonding**

Metals in the solid state have a **giant metallic lattice structure** which is a lattice of **metal cations** and the **sea of delocalised electrons** held together by metallic bonding.

<div id="metallic-lattice-viewer"></div>

**Metallic Bonding** is the electrostatic force of attraction between the **positively charged lattice of metal ions** and the **negatively charged sea of delocalised electrons**.

## **Strength of Metallic Bonding**

### **Factor 1: Number of valence electrons contributed per metal atom**

The larger the number of valence electrons contributed per atom, the greater the number of delocalised electrons, the stronger the metallic bonding.

### **Factor 2: Charge and radius of the metal cation (Charge density)**

The higher the charge and the smaller the radius of the metal cation, the higher is **charge density**, and the stronger the metallic bonding.
