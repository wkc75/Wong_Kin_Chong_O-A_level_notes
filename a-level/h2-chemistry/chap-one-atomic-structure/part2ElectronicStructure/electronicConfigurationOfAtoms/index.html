    Electronic Configuration of Atoms

    So far, we have learned principal quantum shells, subshells and orbitals and their relationship in term of energy level.

    Now we are going to learn how electrons are allocated to the orbitals. The allocation is governed by 3 rules/laws/princiles.

    1: The Aufbau Principle

    Electrons in their ground states occupy orbitals in order of energy levels.

    The orbital with the lowest energy level is always filled first.

    Generally, in an atom, energy level increases with principal quantum number, 1s < 2s < 3s < ...

    In the same principal quantum shell, energy level of subshells increases in the order of s, p, d, f; so 4s < 4p < 4d < 4d < ...

    In the same principal subshell, orbitals are degenerate, so 3px = 3py = 3pz.

    Important

    Take note that 3d is more energetic than 4s when they are both empty. Therefore 4s is filled first 3d.

    Working Example

    Suppose we want to find the electronic configuration of Carbon which has 6 electrons.

    1. We start from the subshell with the lowest energy level which is 1s. Since 1s can accomodate up to 2 electrons, we get \(1s^2\)
    2. Then we are left with 4 electrons to allocate, and the next subshell with the lowest energy level is 2s. Since 2s can accomodate up to 2 electrons, we get \(1s^2 \, 2s^2\)
    3. Finally, we are left with 2 electrons to allocate, and the next subshell with the lowest energy level is 2p. Since 2p can accomodate up to 6 elestrons, we obtain \(1s^2 \, 2s^2 \, 2p^2\)

    2: Hund's Rule of Multiplicity

    When filling subshells that contain more than one orbital with the same energy, each orbital must be singly occupied before electrons are paired.

    Pauli Exclusion Principle

    An orbital cannot hold more than two electrons and the two electrons in the same orbtital must be in opposite spins.

    The direction of the arrow indicates the spin of the electron.

    Exceptions

    Cr

    The electronic configuration of Cr is \(1s^2 \, 2s^2 \, 2p^6 \, 3s^2 \, 3p^6 \, 3d^5 \, 4s^1\) (instead of \(1s^2 \, 2s^2 \, 2p^6 \, 3s^2 \, 3p^6 \, 3d^4 \, 4s^2\))

    Cu

    The electronic configuration of Cr is \(1s^2 \, 2s^2 \, 2p^6 \, 3s^2 \, 3p^6 \, 3d^10 \, 4s^1\) (instead of \(1s^2 \, 2s^2 \, 2p^6 \, 3s^2 \, 3p^6 \, 3d^9 \, 4s^2\))
